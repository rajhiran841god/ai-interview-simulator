# Discovery Interview Guide — Round 1

**Purpose:** Test whether the readiness-gap hypothesis is real. Fact and
recollection only. No solution questions. No pricing questions.

**Duration:** Aim for 25–35 minutes.
**Format:** Live conversation, recorded with consent (notes if declined).
**Interviewer note:** Do not use any competitor AI interview product
before completing your assigned interviews.

---

## Opening (2 min)

- Thank them, explain this is research to understand interview
  preparation, not a pitch for a product.
- Explicitly say there are no wrong answers and you're interested in
  what actually happened, not what should have happened.
- Ask permission to record.

## Core Questions (in order)

1. **"Walk me through how you prepared for your last — or most recent —
   Personal Interview, from when you started until the day of the
   interview."**
   *(Open, behavioral. This replaces "what failed," which invites a
   causal story rather than a recollection.)*

2. **"Tell me about a specific interview — yours or a close friend's —
   that didn't go the way you expected. What happened?"**
   *(Hunting for a concrete, memorable incident.)*

3. **"How did you know when you were 'ready enough' to stop preparing?"**
   *(Tests whether readiness-uncertainty is real and how it's judged.)*

4. **"What did you do when you got a question you weren't expecting?"**
   *(Tests the follow-up/adaptability piece specifically.)*

5. **"After that interview — did you understand why you got the outcome
   you did? How did you find out, if at all?"**
   *(Directly tests the "rejected without understanding why" claim.)*

6. **"What's the most useful feedback anyone gave you during prep? Who
   gave it, and why was it useful?"**
   *(Reveals what good feedback actually looks like to them.)*

7. **"If you had to guess, what actually determined whether you got the
   offer — content of your answers, delivery, resume, luck, something
   else?"**
   *(Falsification lever — tests whether they attribute outcomes to
   readiness at all.)*

8. **"If I had secretly recorded your interview and you watched it back
   afterward, what do you think would surprise you the most?"**
   *(Projective question — bypasses rehearsed narratives. Often surfaces
   things like speaking too fast, interrupting, avoiding eye contact,
   repeating themselves.)*

## For Recruiters / Placement Officers (adapted set)

1. "Walk me through what a typical PI round looks like from your side."
2. "When do candidates usually lose the interview — what's the moment it
   turns?"
3. "What separates a candidate who gets an offer from one who doesn't,
   when both seem similarly qualified on paper?"
4. "What's the most common mistake you see repeated across candidates?"
5. "Do you think candidates generally know why they were rejected? Why
   or why not?"

## Closing

- "Is there anything about interview prep we haven't touched on that you
  think matters?"
- Thank them, confirm how you'll use what they shared.

---

## Explicitly Do NOT Ask (Round 1)

- "Would you use an AI tool that gives adaptive follow-up questions?"
- "Would you pay for X?"
- "Do you think AI feedback would help you?"
- "What would you change about how you prepare?" *(solution-adjacent —
  reserved for Round 2, run as a separate, later conversation, not
  appended to this one)*

Any question that names or implies a solution contaminates this round.
If a participant brings up a solution unprompted, let them talk, note it
as their own volunteered interpretation, but do not follow up with
solution-validation questions in the same sitting.

## Post-Interview (Interviewer Only)

Immediately after each interview, fill out the Interview Repository
entry (see 04_Interview_Repository template) while it's fresh. Do
**not** assign a bottleneck tag yet — that happens only after all 20
interviews are complete, in one sitting, to reduce confirmation bias.
