# Problem Statement — v1 (Frozen)

**Status:** Locked pending Phase 1 discovery results
**Date:** July 2026
**Owner:** Raj

---

## Statement

MBA students preparing for campus placement Personal Interviews often
rely on unstructured preparation methods such as mirrors, friends,
YouTube, ChatGPT, or one or two college-organized mock interviews. These
methods make it difficult for students to understand how they are likely
to perform in an actual interview. As a result, many students continue
using preparation methods that may not improve their communication,
reasoning, or ability to respond to unexpected situations. Consequently,
capable students may underperform in real interviews, receive rejections
without understanding why, and miss internship or placement opportunities
despite having the underlying potential.

## Status of Evidence

This is currently a **working hypothesis**, based on personal experience
and informal conversations with classmates and friends — not yet
validated through structured interviews.

## Scope

- **In scope:** Personal Interview (PI) stage only
- **Out of scope (v1):** Group Discussion (GD), Written Ability Test
  (WAT) — treated as potential future modules, not part of the current
  problem statement
- **Primary population:** MBA students, Indian campus placements
  (initial discovery market: LBSIM + network)
- **Triangulation only, not primary:** engineering students, recent
  graduates, placement officers, recruiters

## Working Definition of "Interview-Ready" (behavioral, not emotional)

A student is interview-ready when they can consistently:
- Understand questions correctly
- Structure answers logically
- Handle unexpected follow-up questions
- Explain experiences using evidence instead of memorized responses
- Communicate clearly and confidently
- Adapt when challenged by the interviewer

This definition is expected to evolve as discovery interviews are
conducted.

## Underlying Hypothesis Structure

The confidence gap and skill gap are believed to reinforce each other
rather than being independent:

1. Students rarely receive objective evaluation →
2. They cannot accurately judge their own readiness →
3. They continue practicing ineffective habits (e.g., memorization) →
4. This produces both continued uncertainty (confidence gap) and
   continued underperformance (skill gap) →
5. The two reinforce each other going into the real interview

## Falsification Condition

This problem statement will be **revised or rejected** if structured
discovery interviews show that the primary bottleneck for MBA placement
candidates is something other than interview readiness — for example:
shortlisting/resume filtering, technical or domain knowledge gaps, or
simply insufficient interview volume/opportunities.

## Research Round Structure

To avoid contaminating problem discovery with solution validation, research is split into separate rounds that must not be mixed in the same conversation:

- **Round 1 (current):** Problem discovery only — what happened, how did
  you prepare, what surprised you. No solution or pricing questions.
- **Round 2 (later, only after Round 1 is synthesized):** Solution
  desirability — what would have helped, what do you wish existed.
- **Round 3 (later still):** Willingness to pay.
